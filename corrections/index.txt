e:T92a,<h1 id="corrections">Corrections</h1>
<p>We get things wrong sometimes. When we do, we fix it in the open — this page explains how, and how you can hold us to it.</p>
<h2 id="how-to-report-an-error">How to report an error</h2>
<p>Email <strong><a href="mailto:contact@fertilityjourney.org">contact@fertilityjourney.org</a></strong>. Tell us the page and what looks wrong — a link to the page and a sentence is plenty. You do not need an account, and you do not need to be an expert. If you&#39;re not sure whether something is an error, tell us anyway; we would rather check.</p>
<h2 id="what-we-do-with-it">What we do with it</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>We read every report.</strong> A real person reviews it against the source the claim is cited to.</li>
<li><strong>If we&#39;re wrong, we correct the page</strong> — and we say so on it. A corrected page carries a dated note: what changed, and when. We don&#39;t quietly edit and pretend it was always right.</li>
<li><strong>If a number changed at the source</strong> (a registry updates its figures, for example), we update the number, refresh the verification date, and note it.</li>
<li><strong>If we can&#39;t reproduce the error,</strong> we&#39;ll write back and explain what we found.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="how-corrections-are-recorded">How corrections are recorded</h2>
<p>Every substantive correction to a medical, cost, or success-rate claim is:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dated</strong> — the page shows when it was corrected.</li>
<li><strong>Specific</strong> — the note says what changed, not just &quot;updated.&quot;</li>
<li><strong>Versioned</strong> — we keep an internal record of the change, so the history of a claim is traceable.</li>
</ul>
<p>Typos and small wording fixes we make quietly. Anything that changes the <em>meaning</em> of a fact, a number, or a recommendation-adjacent statement gets a visible, dated correction note.</p>
<h2 id="why-we-do-it-this-way">Why we do it this way</h2>
<p>Our whole promise is that you can trust what you read here and check us against it. A resource that can&#39;t admit a mistake isn&#39;t independent — it&#39;s just confident. Correcting in the open, with a date, is how we keep the promise real.</p>
<p>Report anything: <strong><a href="mailto:contact@fertilityjourney.org">contact@fertilityjourney.org</a></strong>.</p>
